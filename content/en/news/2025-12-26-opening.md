## Event overview

Date: Saturday, June 6, 2026 — full day, plus an after party
Offline venue: Sapporo Convention Center
Online: Streaming planned

## Format

First things first: the format. We will hold this conference jointly with PHP Conference Hokkaido 2026.

Same day, same place.

A single ticket gets you into both conferences and the after party. We hope attendees will mingle freely across the two communities!

The organizing committees are also working together on preparations, so on the day it will essentially feel like one combined conference.

Since it's a joint event, we'd love to design content that brings together the best of both communities.

## A personal theme for 2026

This time we are running a joint event with PHP Conference Hokkaido. PHP and frontend aren't really that far apart, and I'm genuinely excited that a joint conference between these two areas can happen here in Hokkaido.

In Hokkaido especially, I think there are quite a few cases where both PHP and frontend are used together — frontend ranging from the basics (HTML/CSS/JS) to the view layers offered by various language frameworks, all the way to so-called modern web frontend.

[Why I was able to (and chose to) run Frontend Conference Hokkaido 2025 — event report](https://note.com/_n13u_/n/n2151e54b9830)

In the post above I wrote that, given how many different jobs, businesses, services and systems exist in the world, the Web is hard to separate from any of them. With that backdrop, my personal theme for "Frontend & PHP Conference Hokkaido 2026" is to deliver more modern PHP and frontend knowledge to engineers in Hokkaido.

PHP 8.5 has shipped, and frontend libraries, standards, and best practices keep evolving. The ecosystems around them keep changing too. Every time I hear my fellow Hokkaido native and core staff member tadsan talk about PHPStan, I'm reminded that static analysis in PHP is not only a given but easy to adopt now. I haven't been writing PHP at all lately, so I'm not sure what current best practices are, but…

I'd love everyone to bring PHP and frontend insights from across Japan and across Hokkaido to Frontend & PHP Conference Hokkaido!

For the past two years or so I've also been attending PHP conferences around the country. There's so much you can learn beyond what you'd discover writing PHP alone — talks ranging from straight PHP to "I built a C compiler in PHP" to broader software design discussions.

Doesn't a conference where you can hear PHPers and frontend folks from all over Japan in one place sound great? That's exactly what "Frontend & PHP Conference Hokkaido" aims to be.

Finally —

We hope you'll join us at Frontend & PHP Conference Hokkaido 2026!
